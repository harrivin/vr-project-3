Visualization of Per Capita Income in VR

Description and Motivation

The project presented is a Virtual Reality visualization of the dataset on the GDP per capita income(all races) in the United States from the year 1967-2014. The project begins with a scene that contains a house and a garage, The objects in the house and garage are set to active/inactive with the change in slider, the slider is used to select a particular year and the text shows the GDP per capita of that particular year.The GDP per capita has been adjusted to inflation.

The motivation behind the project is to visualize a dataset and predict how the standard of living(material goods) of an average person living the United States changes from year to year based on the GDP per capita income.

The given experience uses first person perspective, head tracking and stereoscopic rendering to put the user in the Visualization scene. The user can move around the scene using a X-box Joystick(left stick) and can change the value of the slider(years) using "B" and "X" buttons.

The navigation in the virtual environment is in user coordinates.

Dataset Used

The Dataset used is from the Current Population Survey and contains the Per Capita Income of All Races from 1967 - 2014 Link :- http://www.census.gov/hhes/www/income/data/historical/people/2014/p01AR.xls

Insight Goals

1)What would the average household look like for a particular year

2)How the quality of life has changed over time

Intro

The Participants where told what GDP is , what inflation is and how the GDP per capita income is calculated

Gross domestic product (GDP) is the monetary value of all the finished goods and services produced within a country's(US) borders in a specific time period(a year in this case)

Inflation is a sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services in an economy over a period of time. When the price level rises, each unit of currency buys fewer goods and services.

GDP per capita income = GDP/ total population of that specific region

Outro

The users where asked this question after the experience -> Do you think the standard of living goes up if the GDP per capita income increases?

Benefits of it being in VR

  • The user can freely explore the environment and interact with it directly

  • The scale of an object is easier to determine in VR

  • A real environment cannot be easily altered to show new environment changes with the passage of time and change in income

Insights Gained

-the average per capita income was a lot lower than thought and that people got more and nicer things as the years went by

-changes in per capita income are not very volatile but pretty smooth even with the recession in the 2000s

Quantifying Simulator Sickness

We had 6 Men in the age group of 20-25 wear the headset and try out the VR experience(3 VR experience , 3 Intro+Vr Experience + Outro) The Participants were given the Kennedy Simulator Sickness Questionnaire(http://www.cybersickness.org/data/Post.doc) post the VR experience
